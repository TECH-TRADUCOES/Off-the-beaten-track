Off the beaten track

An idiom meaning far from the usual routes, popular places, or commonly known options. It refers to locations, ideas, or activities that are unusual, little-known, or not frequently visited by most people.

• After years of visiting the same crowded tourist cities in Europe, they decided to travel off the beaten track, exploring small mountain villages in northern Spain where locals still cook traditional food and very few foreigners ever go.

• The journalist preferred to report stories off the beaten track, focusing on overlooked rural communities and forgotten environmental problems rather than repeating the same political headlines that dominate the national media every day.

• Instead of studying the most famous philosophers that everyone reads in university courses, she went off the beaten track and wrote her thesis about lesser-known thinkers whose ideas quietly influenced modern debates about science and ethics.

• When we drove through the countryside of Paraíba, we left the main highway and followed a narrow dirt road off the beaten track, eventually discovering a quiet reservoir and a small village that rarely appears on maps.

• The film director is famous for choosing scripts that are completely off the beaten track, telling stories about unusual characters and remote places that most Hollywood productions would probably consider too risky or unfamiliar.

• Many experienced travelers eventually grow tired of crowded landmarks and prefer journeys off the beaten track, where they can meet local residents, learn regional traditions, and see landscapes that mass tourism has not yet transformed.

Synonyms

• off the beaten path

• off the tourist trail

• out of the way

• off the map

• in the middle of nowhere

• away from the crowds
